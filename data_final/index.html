Comparing the Opioid Crisis Under
the Trump and Biden Administrations

Rebecca Raghunath

December 16, 2024

The United States is the largest consumer of opioids in the world. According to the Department of Health, the US makes up 4.4% of the population and consumes over 80% of the world’s opioids.

I gathered data on the provisional overdose death count from 2017-2020 under the first Trump administration and from 2021-2024 under the Biden administration to understand how both administrations were impacted by the opioid crisis.

According to the National Center for Health Statistics, the rate in which Americans have been consuming opioids have increased by nearly triple under the Biden administration. The opioid epidemic skyrocketed all throughout the US, especially California, Texas and Florida—the three most populated states in the US—and continues to escalate in densely populated areas like New York City. Even Ohio, which under the Trump administration had an estimate of 600,000 opioid overdose cases grew by another 100,000 under the Biden administration. Sparsely populated states such as Wyoming have also doubled in opioid overdoses from 2020 to then 2023.

In 2020, a sharp increase of opioid abuse and overdoses occurred in the US. The opioid crisis exacerbated during the Covid-19 pandemic—affecting the end of Trump’s administration and the beginning and mid of the Biden administration.
